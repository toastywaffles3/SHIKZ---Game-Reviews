ARPANET to WWW

Arpanet (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network) was created in 1969. The arpanet is a project of the United States government department of defense to create a communication system that could withstand a nuclear attack. This communications ultimately evolved and became the foreground for the developments of the WWW.

In the 1980s, the National Science Foundation created NSFNET, which was a network that connected smaller networks together. This started the creation of global networks all connected through interconnected networks that bonded the world together ultimately leading to the WWW.

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee created the World Wide Web. It was a way to organize information and access multiple sights of information from the internet to better understand knowledge remotely. This was a major breakthrough as people were able to access others' information and it was able to be shared. Throughout the 1990s and the 2000s, with this the development of search engines came through and social, shopping and networking sites evolved
